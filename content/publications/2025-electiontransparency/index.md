abstract: One of the fundamental requirements of democracy is trust in elections. Still, many recent elections have been marred by allegations of fraud, negatively affecting public confidence. One common concern in such cases is the desire for a more transparent election process, where accusations of tally manipulation or evidence tampering can be more easily verified. To address this issue, we propose Election Transparency, an ecosystem that facilitates the registration, publication, and collaborative verification of electoral data and results. Essentially, the system enables election data to be stored in transparency logs, enabling interested users to easily verify individual entries and large datasets to ensure their integrity. As such, Election Transparency is expected to reduce the costs of auditing procedures. For concreteness, we present Election Transparency in the context of Brazilian elections, although we argue that the core elements of the solution can be adapted to similar scenarios involving electronic voting. We have deployed a prototype with data from a recent, national-scale Brazilian election and evaluated its performance.
